Older Adults and Web Accessibility

Older adults represent a growing segment of internet users and often rely on online services for healthcare, communication, and financial management.

Age-Related Changes

Age-related changes in vision, memory, and processing speed can affect how users interact with websites. These changes do not necessarily indicate disability, but they can make complex or poorly designed interfaces difficult to use.

Accessibility Benefits for Older Adults

Accessible design supports older adults by providing readable text, clear navigation, predictable layouts, and minimal distractions. These features also benefit younger users and individuals without disabilities. As life expectancy increases and digital services become more common, addressing age-related accessibility needs is increasingly important.
