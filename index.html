Project Overview

This website accompanies the paper Reducing Digital Barriers for Cognitive, Visual, and Age-Related Needs. It explores how the evolution of web design has introduced accessibility challenges and demonstrates how inclusive design principles can help reduce those barriers. The site is intentionally designed to follow Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and to provide an example of how accessible web content can benefit individuals with disabilities, older adults, and users with diverse needs.

Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility is essential for equitable access to online services such as healthcare portals, government resources, banking systems, and educational platforms. When websites are not designed with accessibility in mind, individuals with disabilities and older adults may be excluded from participating fully in society.

Who This Website Is For

  • Students and educators studying inclusive design
  • Individuals with cognitive or visual impairments
  • Older adults navigating digital services
  • Web designers and developers learning about accessibility
